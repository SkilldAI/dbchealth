Original Research Abstract

Design Behaviour Codes (DBC) Methodology

DBCs constitute a systematic, codified methodology operationalised through a defined set of 200+ architectural codes. Each code translates evidence from cognitive science and environmental psychology into repeatable design conditions.

"Unlike aesthetic conventions, each DBC specifies a minimum spatial expression and guardrail condition... measurable architectural characteristics that predictably modulate neurobiological processes."

The Two-Brain Logic: Central to the framework is embedding the dual-process model of human cognition [Strack & Deutsch, 2004] into architectural design. The objective is to ensure that the slow, deliberative cognitive system (required for cellular repair) is not overridden by fast, reactive processing caused by environmental stressors.

Pre-Intervention Phase

DBC Home Health Audit

Before executing any architectural intervention, certified DBC auditors conduct a rigorous, data-driven spatial assessment. Using calibrated environmental sensors, we quantify the precise neurobiological tax the residence is inflicting on the recovering patient.

1

EHR Vulnerability Sync

The audit team cross-references the patient's Electronic Health Record (EHR). We map specific clinical vulnerabilities (e.g., cardiac instability) to their corresponding environmental stressors (e.g., acoustic shock).

2

IoT Sensory Mapping

Auditors deploy decibel meters and lux sensors across the primary recovery zones. We map baseline infiltration rates to identify the exact thresholds triggering autonomic arousal.

3

Spatial Friction Auditing

A physical assessment of circulation routes, identifying trip hazards, 'pinch points', and abrupt transition zones that impose a continuous System 1 cognitive tax on the patient.

Physical Application of DBCs

Not interior decoration. We execute rapid, medical-grade architectural interventions governed by strict, measurable guardrails to down-regulate the autonomic nervous system.

DBC-S14

Sensory Calibration

Target Mechanism: Mitigation of auditory/visual shock causing autonomic arousal.

Physical Intervention: Targeted acoustic sealing to primary external apertures. Eradication of >4000K LED flicker. Installation of 2700K circadian-aligned dimmable luminaires in the rest zone.

DBC-T02

Threshold Gradients

Target Mechanism: Prevention of physiological shock to pupillary and nervous systems upon zone transition.

Physical Intervention: Erection of rapid-deploy semi-permeable spatial screens. Establishing 'buffer zones' to force physical pacing. Stepping down lux levels between entryways and beds.

DBC-C09

Circulation Continuity

Target Mechanism: Removal of spatial friction demanding continuous micro-decisions (System 1 cognitive tax).

Physical Intervention: Re-routing of primary furniture massing based on patient mobility vectors. Removal of floor friction points. Implementation of visual 'Rhythmic Structural Repetition'.
