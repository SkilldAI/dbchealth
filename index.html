Original Research Abstract

Design Behaviour Codes (DBC)
Methodology

DBCs constitute a systematic, codified methodology operationalised through a defined set of 200+ architectural codes. Each code translates evidence from cognitive science and environmental psychology into repeatable, auditable design conditions.

"Unlike aesthetic conventions, each DBC specifies a minimum spatial expression and guardrail condition... measurable architectural characteristics that predictably modulate neurobiological processes."

The Two-Brain Logic: Central to the framework is operationally embedding the dual-process model of human cognition[Strack & Deutsch, 2004] into architectural design. The objective is to ensure that the slow, deliberative cognitive system (required for physiological rest and cellular repair) is not overridden by fast, reactive processing caused by environmental stressors.

Physical Application of DBCs

Not interior decoration. We execute rapid, medical-grade architectural interventions governed by strict, measurable guardrails to down-regulate the autonomic nervous system.

DBC-S14

Sensory Load Calibration

Target Mechanism: Mitigation of auditory/visual shock causing autonomic arousal.

Physical Intervention: Application of targeted acoustic sealing to primary external apertures. Eradication of >4000K LED flicker. Installation of 2700K circadian-aligned dimmable luminaires in the primary rest zone.

Metrics: Ambient dB reduction to < 45dB. Glare elimination.
DBC-T02

Threshold Gradients

Target Mechanism: Prevention of physiological shock to pupillary and nervous systems upon zone transition.

Physical Intervention: Erection of rapid-deploy semi-permeable spatial screens. Establishing calculated 'buffer zones' to force physical pacing. Stepping down lux levels between entryways and recovery areas.

Metrics: Gradual pupillary adaptation. Elimination of startle reflex.
DBC-C09

Circulation Continuity

Target Mechanism: Removal of spatial friction demanding continuous micro-decisions (System 1 cognitive tax).

Physical Intervention: Re-routing of primary furniture massing based on patient mobility vectors. Removal of floor-level friction points. Implementation of visual 'Rhythmic Structural Repetition' to signal clear, safe pathways.

Metrics: Navigational friction reduced. Fall risk minimized.
