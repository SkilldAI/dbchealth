The Two-Brain Logic

Central to the DBC framework is operationally embedding the dual-process model of human cognition into architectural design. The environment dictates whether the slow, deliberative cognitive system (healing/repair) is overridden by fast, reactive processing (survival/stress).

Background and Efficacy: The built environment exerts continuous influences on physiology. Design Behaviour Codes (DBCs) constitute a systematic methodology operationalised through 200+ architectural codes. These codes translate evidence from cognitive science, behavioural economics, environmental psychology, and applied physics into repeatable, auditable design conditions.

Unlike aesthetic conventions, each DBC specifies a minimum spatial expression and guardrail condition. These are measurable characteristics that predictably modulate neurobiological processes, shaping human cognition and emotion.

Key Architectural Modulators

  • 1. Sensory Load Calibration: Managing auditory and visual stimuli to prevent autonomic overload.
  • 2. Threshold Gradients: Creating transitions that allow physiological adaptation between spatial zones.
  • 3. Circulation Continuity: Removing spatial friction that demands continuous micro-decisions (System 1 taxes).
  • 4. Rhythmic Structural Repetition: Providing predictable spatial patterns that signal environmental safety.

Operationalizing for HomeHealth: By deploying a subset of these DBCs into the post-discharge residential environment, we can artificially induce the System 2 restorative state. This is not interior decoration; it is medical-grade environmental modification designed to lower heart rate variability and sustain the clinical gains achieved within the hospital setting.
